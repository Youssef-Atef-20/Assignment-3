It all started with a simple thought. My passion for fashion coupled with my interest in online marketing led me to create Trendify Facebook page; my aim was to sell clothes without large profit margins or establishing my online brand — I mainly wanted to test the waters in digital marketing. At first, engaging with customers and marketing my products was challenging; I quickly learned how to create content that engages people, which turned out to be the most important part of marketing. I began with a limited-stock photoshoot of casual wear sets and accompanied them with captions that were easy to remember. People slowly started noticing the pieces I sold, and their likes and comments motivated me to do better. After a while, my page really began to grow after I started using Facebook Ads. Customer satisfaction allows for a deeper understanding of target audiences, combining them with special themed promotions creates engaging media. Trendify’s purpose has shifted over time; what once started as an idea now serves as my fun experiment, learning journey, and along the way has made me a proud, albeit modest businesswoman. As of right now, I have an e-commerce business, though I’m still learning, it’s predicted to bloom Trendify was just the start and it makes this e-commerce adventure much more thrilling.
